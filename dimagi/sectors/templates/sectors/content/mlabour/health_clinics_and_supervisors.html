{% blocktrans %} Health Clinics and Supervisors {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Problem" %}: {% blocktrans %} Health clinics receive partographs that are filled out post-labor; data takes too long to reach decision makers; up to date statistics are not always available. {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %} Real-time data reporting enables supervisors to detect data irregularities and observe whether recommended actions were taken, including identifying providers whose clinical practices in labor monitoring and management need improvement. {% endblocktrans %}
  • {% blocktrans %} Algorithms are capable of detecting post-labor use, for increased accountability. {% endblocktrans %}
  • {% blocktrans %} Live partograph reports enable district health centers and Ministries of Health to view and monitor complete labor records, including the full computer-plotted partograph. {% endblocktrans %}
  • {% blocktrans %} Monthly and quarterly reports allow health administrators to assess labor monitoring, timely provision of care for abnormal labors, and maternal and fetal health outcomes. {% endblocktrans %}
  • {% blocktrans %} Key alerts on emergency action can be sent to supervisors, and/or referral facilities, who can provide immediate back-up support on appropriate next steps. {% endblocktrans %}
