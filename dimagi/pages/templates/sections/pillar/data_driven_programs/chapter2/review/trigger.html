{% blocktrans %} Establish Data-Action Triggers {% endblocktrans %}

{% include "partials/wistia_popover.html" with wistia_id='rbl3icvr1u' play_icon_medium=True show_image=True %}

{% blocktrans %} Actually acting on what your reports tell you becomes much easier if you take the time to plan ahead. Set ranges for what “normal” data looks like and agree ahead of time on what actions should be triggered when normal thresholds are crossed. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} When discussing the potential actions, you first need to know what you can and cannot change in your program, keeping in mind both the scope and scale of any potential actions: {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %} What does your application development time look like? Could you release a new version of the app, if needed? {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %} How is your project set up to contact frontline workers and give them assistance if they’re having a hardware or software issue? {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %} How flexible are your frontline workers? Could you send them to alternative locations or catchment areas? {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %} How flexible is the budget? Do you have extra funds you could allocate to a new activity? Could you reallocate funds from one activity to another? {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %} Are there partner organizations working in the same space that could provide add-on services to your beneficiaries, if needed? {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} Once you establish these data-action triggers, the whole process becomes much simpler: Just check your automated reports every day, ready to take action. And be flexible! You can always change the parameters or actions themselves. {% endblocktrans %}
