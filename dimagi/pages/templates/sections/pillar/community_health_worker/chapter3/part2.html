{% blocktrans %} Ethiopia's CHW Program {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} Ethiopia, one of the countries analyzed by USAID, is an unlikely success story. In one of the poorest countries in the world, the Ministry of Health has hired and trained 40,000 community health workers to provide essential health services around the country, combating child mortality, HIV, malaria, and TB. Since 1990, child mortality has decreased by 70%, HIV infections by 90%, malaria deaths by 75% and TB deaths by 64%. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} While smaller, non-governmental organizations have been implementing the community health worker model on a local level with documented success, Ethiopia stands as an example of how even the most underfunded ministries of health can help improve health outcomes at a national level. {% endblocktrans %}
