{% blocktrans %} Community health workers can take on a number of different roles in their community, including responsibilities for different aspects of service delivery and the health system. The core responsibilities of a traditional community health worker role are to: {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %} Deliver health information and services (e.g. first aid, vaccines, diagnoses, counseling, etc.) to their local populations {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %} Improve the health-seeking behavior of their patients, employing their unique understanding of the situations and challenges they face {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %} Make home visits to patients unable to visit clinics or health facilities (e.g. high-risk illnesses, pregnant women, the elderly, etc.) {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %} Help their patients develop an understanding of and navigate available health resources (e.g. clinics, insurance, etc.) {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %} Facilitate communication, connections, and care between patients and the existing health system {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %} Collect data on the habits, conditions, and demographics of their community to help inform the improvement of local health services and policy {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% blocktrans %} Act as an advocate and representative of their patients and community to the health system to educate stakeholders and providers on health topics relevant to their community {% endblocktrans %}
